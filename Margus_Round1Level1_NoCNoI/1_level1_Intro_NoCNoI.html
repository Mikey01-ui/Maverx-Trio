The Challenge

You've just joined a growing tech company as a Data Analyst. On your first day, you discover that the organization has accumulated datasets across multiple departments, but there's confusion about who owns what data.

Your manager has tasked you with organizing the company's data landscape by correctly assigning each dataset to its rightful departmental owner. This isn't just about storage locations—it's about establishing clear accountability and governance.

Your Mission
Analyze dataset metadata, lineage, and ownership information to assign 10-15 datasets to their correct departments. Each correct placement demonstrates your understanding of data governance principles.
Icon

Learning Objectives

  • 1
    Identify Data Ownership
    Distinguish between data owners and data users based on accountability and stewardship.
  • 2
    Analyze Metadata
    Evaluate data source, lineage, and reliability indicators to make informed decisions.
  • 3
    Apply Governance Principles
    Understand that ownership is based on responsibility, not just usage or storage location.
  • 4
    Make Decisions Under Pressure
    Practice critical thinking and decision-making with time constraints.
